## Visual Identity and Token Reference

> This document defines the complete visual identity for The Bridge Hub.
> It replaces Chat 06 scope. All design decisions are locked.
> Chat 07 builds directly from this document + the wireframe boards.
> Last updated: June 2026

---

## Brand feeling

A quiet field guide for the nervous system.

Not medical. Not spa. Not quiz app. Not therapy worksheet.

The visual world is soft, editorial, grounded, and safe. Every screen
should answer one hidden question in the user's body:

"Am I safe enough to keep going?"

The style sits between: storybook map, clinical trust, soft nature
journal, and cinematic calm.

## Typography

### Title font — Cormorant Garamond (serif)

Use for: page titles, section transitions, emotional headlines,
results headings.

| Context | Size (mobile) | Weight | Letter spacing | Line height |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Large titles | 42-56px | 400-500 | 0 to 0.02em | 1.05-1.15 |
| Section titles | 38-48px | 400-500 | 0 to 0.02em | 1.05-1.15 |
| Headings | 20-28px | 400-500 | 0 | 1.2-1.3 |

### Body font — Inter (sans-serif)

Use for: body copy, buttons, form labels, question text, disclaimers.

| Context | Size | Weight | Letter spacing | Line height |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Body | 15-17px | 400 | 0 | 1.45-1.6 |
| Small text | 12-13px | 400 | 0 | 1.5 |
| Labels | 11-12px | 500 | 0.12em | 1.4 |
| Uppercase labels | 11-12px | 500 | 0.12em | uppercase |

### Logo text — Cormorant Garamond or Libre Baskerville

Size: 13-16px. Weight: 600. Letter spacing: 0.18-0.25em.
Text: THE BRIDGE HUB. Small arc underneath as brand mark.

## Section worlds

### Section 1 — The Load (PSS-10)
Theme: stone, pressure, responsibility, held weight.
Visual: warm valley with rocks, dry path, low hills.
Palette: Stone + Sage.
Copy: "This section looks at the pressure your body may have learned to hold."

### Section 2 — The Fog (PHQ-8)
Theme: mood, heaviness, low colour, tiredness.
Visual: mist over hills, pale sun, soft lake fading into distance.
Palette: Mist + Stone + pale Sage.
Copy: "We'll explore how life has been feeling lately."

### Section 3 — The Body Room (MAIA-2)
Theme: body awareness, numbness, signals, tension.
Visual: quiet room, soft window light, blanket, plants, candle, stillness.
Palette: Sage + Cream + warm light.
Copy: "This section explores what your body has been noticing, holding, and signalling."

### Section 4 — The Weight You Carry (PCL-5)
Theme: difficult experiences, old alarm, what still echoes.
Visual: cool lake, cloudy sky, reeds, water holding reflection.
Palette: Faded Blue + Mist + Stone.
Copy: "We'll look at what you've been carrying and how it shows up."

### Section 5 — The Weather Inside (PID-5-SF)
Theme: emotional patterns, self trust, reaction, protection.
Visual: soft sky, shifting clouds, warm light breaking through.
Palette: Muted Mauve + soft peach + Mist.
Copy: "This section explores your emotional patterns, protection, and what changes the weather inside."

## Breathing overlay

Background: Dark Room #1F2925 with soft grain + subtle vignette.
Breathing circle outer: thin line #BEC2A9 low opacity.
Inner circle: radial gradient #CAD0B6 to #6F796C.
Glow: soft sage behind circle.
Text: Cream, Cormorant Garamond 36-44px. Instruction: Inter 15px 85% opacity.
Close: circle outline top right, 32-40px, border #CFC4B5 at 60%.
Bottom button: transparent dark bg, cream border, radius 12-14px.
Label in UI: "Take a breath" (not "breathing exercise").

---

## Background and texture

Never flat white. Main background: Warm Paper #F6F0E6.
Add very light paper texture at 8-15% opacity.
Texture should be almost invisible — just stops the screen feeling digital.

Motifs: soft watercolour hills, mist, botanical line art (1.25px, sage/stone,
low opacity), circles for breath/completion/confirmation.

---

## Layout rules

Mobile padding: 24px outer, 16-20px inside cards.
Top logo area: 60-80px. Headline: centred or slightly above centre.
Buttons: near bottom, not cramped. Footer disclaimers: small, soft, centred.
Max content width desktop: 640px centred.

Avoid: crowded text, multiple CTAs, sharp corners, strong shadows,
bright white, clinical blue, progress bars that feel like a test timer.

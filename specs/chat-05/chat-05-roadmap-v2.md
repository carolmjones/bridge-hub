## Language standard established in report v5

The following principles are now locked and must be applied to all 88 blocks:

### Voice and register
- Warm, direct, plain language
- Never clinical labels as the lead — clinical weight appears after human framing
- No em dashes anywhere
- Nervous system framing throughout: adaptation, protection, survival, load, capacity
- Screening tool framing: "may", "appears", "suggests" throughout interpretive text
- Not a standalone intervention — language supports, does not prescribe

### Specific replacements locked
| Original clinical language | User-facing replacement |
|---|---|
| Major depressive disorder | Clinically significant depressive symptoms |
| Clinically significant (client-facing chart labels) | Strongly elevated |
| PTSD | Not used directly — "trauma-related symptoms" or "what you have been carrying" |
| Diagnosis | Not used |
| Disorder | Not used |
| "Is consistent with X disorder" | "Is commonly associated with X symptoms" |
| "The respondent" | "You" |
| "This person" | "You" |
| Third person throughout | Second person throughout |
| Em dashes | Commas or full stops |
| Deficit framing as lead | Adaptation framing first, research second |

### What must be added to each block
- Hedging language: "may", "appears", "suggests" on all interpretive claims
- Nervous system framing where relevant
- The phrase "This report cannot replace a conversation with a qualified professional"
  appears in the footer and opening — individual blocks do not repeat it

### What must be kept
- All scientific citations and references
- All band-specific clinical context
- The structural integrity of what each block describes
- Biological claims (Caroline confirmed these stay)

## Phase 1 — Layer 2 user-facing language pass (HIGHEST PRIORITY)

**What this is:**
A systematic rewrite of all 88 Layer 2 blocks from clinical register
to user-facing register, applying the language standard above.

**Why this matters:**
These blocks are the backbone of the client report. They are pre-written,
not AI-generated, so they must be correct before the tool launches.
Every client who completes the screening will read these blocks.

**How many blocks:**
- PSS-10: 5 blocks (PSS-LOW, PSS-MODERATE, PSS-HIGH, PSS-HELPLESSNESS-HIGH,
  PSS-EFFICACY-LOW)
- PHQ-8: 7 blocks (PHQ-NONE, PHQ-MILD, PHQ-MODERATE, PHQ-MODERATELY-SEVERE,
  PHQ-SEVERE, PHQ-SOMATIC-HIGH, PHQ-COGNITIVE-HIGH)
- MAIA-2: approximately 20 blocks (3 bands × 5 subscales + additional flag blocks)
- PCL-5: approximately 11 blocks (5 bands + 6 cluster/algorithm blocks)
- PID-5-SF: approximately 45 blocks (3 bands × 3 domains + 3 bands × 12 facets)
- Total: approximately 88 blocks

**Output:**
A new document: `chat-05-layer2-user-facing-v1.md`
This replaces the client-facing use of Chat 03's original blocks.
Chat 03's original blocks remain as the therapist briefing source.

**Working method:**
One instrument at a time. Caroline approves each instrument before the next begins.
